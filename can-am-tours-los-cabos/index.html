FAQ

COMMON QUESTIONS

What Can-Am models are used on tours in Los Cabos?+
Baja Curated operates 2025/2026 Can-Am Maverick models including the Trail, DS Max, and Maverick R — all purchased directly from BRP as an Authorized Dealer. The specific model assigned depends on the tour and group size, but all are current-year and factory-maintained.
Is Baja Curated really an Authorized Can-Am BRP Dealer?+
Yes. Baja Curated holds official Authorized Dealer status from BRP (Bombardier Recreational Products), Can-Am's parent company. This is the same certification held by Can-Am vehicle dealerships worldwide. You can verify this through BRP's official dealer locator.
Can I drive the Can-Am myself or do I have to ride along?+
You drive. Unlike ride-along services where a staff member operates the vehicle, Baja Curated gives you full control of the Can-Am Maverick. Your bilingual guide leads the route in a separate vehicle. A valid driver's license is required. Minimum age: 18 years.
How much does a Can-Am tour in Los Cabos cost?+
Baja Curated tours range from $550 USD (3-hour Desert Intro) to $2,695 USD (16-hour Extreme expedition). All prices are per vehicle, not per person — meaning a Can-Am fits 2 passengers and both share that price. Price includes vehicle, guide, full safety gear, radios, and hydration.
What is the difference between a Can-Am tour and an ATV tour in Cabo?+
Can-Am Mavericks (also called UTV or side-by-side vehicles) seat 2-4 passengers in a roll cage with harnesses, steering wheel, and suspension built for high-speed terrain. ATVs are single-rider quad bikes. Can-Ams are significantly more powerful, comfortable, and safe for extended off-road expeditions — especially at Baja's terrain scale.
